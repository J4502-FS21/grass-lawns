Timeline of grass lawn history

Medieval France and Britain

Laundes, a middle English word for an opening in the woods, surrounded castles in Europe to aid defense. Lawns also appeared in village "commons" and served as areas for sheep and cattle to graze

16th Century Renaissance

Wealthy elites intentionally cultivated lawns out of chamomile or thyme.

17th Century England

Wealthy land owners begin to cultivate short lawns that were scythed with human labor.

Colonial era in North America

Immigrants from Northern Europe brought grass seeds with them to North America and lawns became a popular venue for golf and bowling, thanks to the Scottish.

1830

Edwin Budding invented the first lawnmower.

1948-1942

Levittown, New York, was built. It was the first American suburb to come with pre-installed lawns.
